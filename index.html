  • 05.12.22
    Nikolay Vereshchagin - Simultaneous minimization of complexities

    The initial question: consider three strings \(x,y,z\). Then the minimal program \(p\) that gets \(x\) from \(z\) has length \(C(x|z)\) by definition, and the minimal program \(q\) that gets \(y\) from \(z\) has length \(C(y|z)\). Is it possible to find a pair \((p,q)\) of programs with these properties (of the minimal or approximately minimal lengths mapping \(z\) to \(x/y\) such that complexity of the pair \((p,q)\) is close to the minimal possible (i.e., to \(C(x,y|z))\)? The answer is (somewhat surpisingly) negative, and the proof of this result and some related ones will be explained

  • 24.10.22
    Alexander Shen - Kraft, Chaitin and Barmpalias revisited

    Kraft-Chaitin lemma provides a memory allocation algorithm: it gets a sequence of integers \(l_1, l_2,\ldots\) such that \(\sum 2^{-l_i} \le 1\), and returns (in an online fashion) a sequence of incomparable strings \(x_1,x_2,\ldots\) of the corresponding lengths.

    George Barmpalias generalized this construction and proved and hierarchical version of this lemma: now the request \(l_i\) can be not only of the form "give me some new space", but also of the form "give me some space inside the space allocated for earlier request". This is useful when we want to construct, for some sequence \(\alpha\), its compressed version \(\beta\) when the prefixes of \(\beta\) can be decoded to prefixes of \(\alpha\). There are also some results about possible limits of that type of compression.

  • {3,10}.10.22
    Nikolay Vereshchagin - Algorithmic problems as games.

    Every algorithmic problem can be understood a certain two-player game with perfect information. The decidability of the problem means the existence of an algorithm computing a winning strategy in the game. Reductions between problems can also be understood as games of the same type. This approach was developed in the papers of Blass and Japaridze and is an alternative to Medvedev's approach, according to which an algorithmic problems can be identified as a family of total functions and its solution is an algorithm that computes a function from the family.
